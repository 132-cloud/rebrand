# Creative Brief — Primary Core

**Page:** /solutions/primary-core
**In one line:** The Primary Core page makes the case for replacing a legacy core or launching a new institution on Nymbus — with a defined migration path and managed operations from day one.

## What this page is for
Bottom-of-funnel for institutions that have decided a core replacement is necessary or are building a de novo institution. This is the highest-stakes decision on the site — the page needs to make the case that Nymbus is a credible, de-risked path. Primary action: book a demo.

## Who it's for
Primary: CEOs, COOs, and CIOs at established community banks and credit unions actively evaluating a core replacement — they're on a 1–3 year planning horizon. Secondary: founders and organizers of de novo banks or credit unions building their technology stack from scratch. Both arrive having already concluded that their current foundation is a constraint; they're evaluating which modern core to choose.

## What we're communicating
- "A modern app on a rigid core still moves at the core's pace." — the problem is the foundation, not the front end
- Two paths covered: core replacement for established institutions and de novo launch for new ones
- Migration is planned and de-risked: configuration, integration, conversion, testing, training, and launch readiness on a defined path — there is a date
- Managed Services can operate from day one — no need to staff the operation before going live
- Cross-link to parallel core for institutions not yet ready for full replacement
- Three testimonials: Thomas Senecal (PeoplesBank — "go-live affirmed our decision"), Ami Iceman (MSUFCU), Sara Dolan (MSUFCU — "Pillur" migration quote)

## Tone & feel
Authoritative and reassuring, in the "Built for Builders" brand chapter. This is the most consequential product page on the site — it speaks to institutions making a multi-year, institution-defining decision. Confidence without over-promising. Specificity over generality. The migration plan section must feel concrete, not aspirational.

## Must-haves / non-negotiables
- Four sequential split-panel feature blocks: Replace your core / Launch a new institution / A migration built on a plan / Run the operation from day one
- Section IDs preserved (#core-replacement, #de-novo) — these are deep-linked from other pages including the home hero prompt routes
- Two-card path callout (Core replacement / De novo) with cross-link to parallel core
- Full FAQ (7 questions) verbatim — includes the "what if we want to launch something first?" parallel core handoff question
- Logo strip with "25 digital banks launched and cores deployed on Nymbus"
- Three testimonials all verbatim

## Interactive / visual elements
- Four split-panel sections: alternating layout; image placeholders — final designs should show: (1) modern core dashboard/UI, (2) de novo launch timeline or milestone graphic, (3) implementation/migration roadmap visual, (4) managed services operations illustration
- The migration roadmap visual is the most important asset to develop — it needs to make the "defined path" claim tangible

## What to avoid
- Don't make the migration sound easy — it's a serious undertaking; the page earns trust by being honest about what's involved and showing that Nymbus has a plan for it
- Don't position de novo as a side note — it's a distinct audience with a distinct journey
- Avoid generic "modern" without specifying what moves: products, rates, segments without vendor roadmap waits
- Em-dashes
