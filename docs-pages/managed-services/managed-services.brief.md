# Creative Brief — Managed Services

**Page:** /managed-services
**In one line:** The Managed Services page sells the operational layer behind the Nymbus platform — the team that runs contact center, onboarding, fraud, compliance, and back-office behind your brand.

## What this page is for
Mid-to-bottom funnel for institutions evaluating whether they can launch or scale a banking model without building an internal operations team. The core argument: Nymbus doesn't just deploy technology — it runs the operation too. Primary action: contact an expert about managed services.

## Who it's for
Primary: CEOs and COOs at banks and credit unions evaluating whether their institution has the operational capacity to support a new digital brand or expanded product line. They're asking "who runs the back office?" Secondary: existing Nymbus platform customers evaluating whether to add managed services to their contract. They arrive with operational capacity concerns, not technology concerns.

## What we're communicating
- Nymbus runs the operation, not just the software — the same team that built it operates it
- You keep control: you set the thresholds, you own the regulatory responsibility, Nymbus executes inside your rules
- Six operational domains covered: contact center, onboarding, back-office, risk/compliance, fraud, and business support
- Every application is decisioned in seconds via Socure ID+ auto-routing — no application sits in a queue
- Reporting keeps you current monthly; enhanced reporting available as add-on

## Tone & feel
Operational and reassuring, in the "Built for Builders" brand chapter. The audience has been burned by outsourced operations before — they need specificity, not promises. "We run the work. You keep control." is the governing tension of the page and should be felt in every section.

## Must-haves / non-negotiables
- "We don't just deploy it. We run it." — the headline is the brand promise for this page; must stay
- Shared-services model section with three cards (You set the thresholds / You keep regulatory ownership / Automation runs inside your rules)
- Seven operational domains listed by name in the team grid
- Digital Onboarding section with Socure ID+ routing detail — this specificity builds credibility
- Ongoing Operations three-card grid (back office / fraud / compliance)
- Full FAQ (10 questions) — the most detailed FAQ on the site; must be complete and verbatim
- The placeholder stat ("TBD") in the proof strip needs a real number before launch

## Interactive / visual elements
- Proof strip: three stat blocks at top — the "TBD" third stat is a flagged gap
- Go Deeper section at page bottom: three content cards (Whitepaper / Podcast / Whitepaper) — these link to /insights/?tag=managed-services; actual content needed before launch

## What to avoid
- Don't make this sound like a call center outsourcing pitch — managed services is a full operating model, not just support
- Avoid implying Nymbus makes compliance decisions — the page is careful about this distinction; preserve it
- Em-dashes
