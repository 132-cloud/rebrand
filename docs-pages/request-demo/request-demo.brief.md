# Creative Brief — Request a Demo

**Page:** /request-demo
**In one line:** The primary bottom-of-funnel conversion page — a focused demo request form paired with a clear statement of what the conversation will cover.

## What this page is for
Bottom-of-funnel conversion. This page exists for one purpose: get a qualified prospect to submit their details so the Nymbus team can follow up. Every element on the page should serve that goal. Primary action: submit the demo request form.

## Who it's for
Primary: Decision-makers at banks, credit unions, and fintechs who are ready for a sales conversation — they've evaluated Nymbus, understand the platform at a high level, and want to see it applied to their specific situation. Secondary: visitors routed here from CTAs throughout the site who may need a light reminder of what the demo covers. They arrive with intent; the page should confirm it and get out of the way.

## What we're communicating
- This is a strategy conversation, not just a product demo — "See how Nymbus fits your strategy"
- Three strategy areas covered: growth, modernization, operating model
- Specific paths shown: primary core, parallel core, managed operations — signals scope and relevance
- The form is short (6 required fields + 1 optional) — low friction on purpose
- Success message is intentionally brief: "Thanks. A member of the Nymbus team will follow up shortly."

## Tone & feel
Direct and efficient, in the "Built to Move" brand chapter. The copy left of the form should validate the visitor's intent without over-explaining. "See how Nymbus fits your strategy" is an invitation, not a pitch — preserve that register. The form should feel fast.

## Must-haves / non-negotiables
- Two-column layout: copy left, form right — matches the /contact page pattern
- Company type selector: Bank / Credit Union / Fintech / Other — required for lead routing
- Company website field (URL) rather than company name — used for account enrichment
- State field — required for regional routing and compliance context
- Success state replaces form without page reload; message is brief and confident
- The page should not require a phone number — intentional; lower barrier than /lp/request-demo

## Interactive / visual elements
- Form with inline validation; success state replaces form on submit
- Hero uses shared video background (footer-cta-bg.webm) — carry through to redesign
- No additional visual elements needed on this page — the form is the focus

## What to avoid
- Don't add proof stats, testimonials, or feature lists to this page — visitors here already know enough to submit; additional copy creates friction
- Don't require a phone number — the current form deliberately omits it
- Avoid a long confirmation message — "Thanks." is the right length
- Em-dashes
