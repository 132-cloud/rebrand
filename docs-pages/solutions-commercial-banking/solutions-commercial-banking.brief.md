# Creative Brief — Commercial Banking

**Page:** /solutions/commercial-banking
**In one line:** The Commercial Banking page targets institutions losing treasury and cash management relationships to larger banks, positioning Nymbus as a purpose-built commercial platform deployable without a core replacement.

## What this page is for
Mid-to-bottom-funnel for institutions with commercial banking ambitions constrained by a retail core. The page covers five capability areas (onboarding, controls, payments, fraud, operations) and three business segments (SMB through commercial). Primary action: book a demo.

## Who it's for
Primary: CEOs and commercial banking heads at community banks and credit unions watching multi-million dollar treasury relationships migrate to larger institutions. They know the problem: their tools were built for retail accounts, not commercial treasury. Secondary: CFOs and COOs evaluating the operational investment required to support commercial clients. They arrive with a competitive urgency — they're already losing relationships.

## What we're communicating
- Treasury and cash management relationships leave because retail cores weren't built for commercial depth — Nymbus solves this directly
- Five capability areas: multi-entity onboarding, treasury controls (roles/approvals/limits), commercial payments (real-time ACH, wires, NACHA, Positive Pay), fraud protection (ACH + Check Positive Pay), and managed operations
- Launch on a parallel core without disrupting the primary institution
- The relationship grows from SMB to full commercial and treasury on one platform
- Three testimonials: Stephen Owen (First Entertainment CU), Thomas Senecal (PeoplesBank), Ami Iceman (MSUFCU)

## Tone & feel
Technical and commercially credible, in the "Built Without Limits" brand chapter. The commercial banking audience includes treasury professionals and CFOs who evaluate platforms on depth of controls and payment rails, not consumer experience. Lead with capability specifics — NACHA upload, beneficial ownership, IOLTA, sweep accounts — these details signal that Nymbus was actually built for this.

## Must-haves / non-negotiables
- Five sequential split-panel feature blocks — each has a distinct capability focus and a named link to a deeper resource
- Three-tier segment cards (Small business / Growth firms / Commercial businesses) with specific treasury depth called out for the commercial tier
- Full FAQ (8 questions) verbatim — includes the specific regulatory nuance around KYB and beneficial ownership
- Logo strip with "25 digital banks launched and cores deployed on Nymbus" social proof line
- "Explore small business banking" cross-link to /solutions/business-banking/

## Interactive / visual elements
- Five split-panel sections: alternating text-left / text-right; all image slots currently placeholders — final designs should show treasury UI, payments flows, or controls dashboards
- Logo strip: 5 institution logos at consistent scale, grayscale, 50% opacity

## What to avoid
- Don't conflate commercial banking with small business banking — the commercial tier has distinct treasury, IOLTA, sweep, and multi-entity requirements that must stay differentiated
- Avoid soft language around fraud and compliance — Positive Pay and beneficial ownership are hard requirements for this audience, not nice-to-haves
- Em-dashes
