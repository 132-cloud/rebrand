# Creative Brief — Personal Banking

**Page:** /solutions/personal-banking
**In one line:** The Personal Banking page makes the case for launching a modern retail experience that earns primary account status — covering account opening, mobile experience, money movement, engagement, and operations.

## What this page is for
Mid-to-bottom-funnel for institutions whose retail accounts are losing primary status to neobanks and mobile-first fintechs. The page addresses the full consumer journey from account opening through ongoing primary relationship. Primary action: book a demo.

## Who it's for
Primary: CEOs and digital banking leads at community banks and credit unions watching direct deposit and daily transaction volume shift to Chime, SoFi, and similar apps. They know the experience gap; they need to understand how quickly and at what cost it can be closed. Secondary: product and operations leaders evaluating the managed services model for retail. They arrive with competitive urgency — the primary relationship is eroding.

## What we're communicating
- Consumers are moving their primary banking to apps that feel faster — the window to act is now
- "Earn primary, not just an open account." — the goal isn't the account, it's the relationship
- Five capability areas: digital account opening, mobile-first experience (iOS/Android/web), money movement (ACH, bill pay, P2P, mobile deposit), engagement tools (insights, rewards, credit monitoring), managed operations
- Parallel core deployment available — no need to replace the primary core to launch
- Three customer lifecycle stages: new accounts, everyday banking, primary relationships

## Tone & feel
Consumer-forward and relationship-oriented, in the "Built for Builders" brand chapter applied to the retail audience. Unlike the commercial pages, this one should feel warm and human while still being strategically grounded. "Earn primary" is the governing phrase — everything on the page serves that outcome.

## Must-haves / non-negotiables
- Five sequential split-panel feature blocks in page order: Account Opening / Digital Experience / Money Movement / Engagement / Managed Operations
- Three-card customer lifecycle grid (New account holders / Everyday banking customers / Primary relationships)
- Full FAQ (8 questions) verbatim — includes parallel core deployment, channel coverage, and managed services questions
- Three testimonials: Stephen Owen (First Entertainment CU / "digital-first experience"), Jill Castilla (Citizens Bank of Edmond), Aleda DeMaria (PeoplesBank)
- Logo strip at top — "Trusted by top financial institutions"

## Interactive / visual elements
- Five split-panel sections: alternating layout; all image placeholders — final designs should show mobile app UI screens, account opening flow, engagement dashboard
- The engagement section in particular benefits from showing the in-app experience: cash flow insights, credit score widget, rewards — these are visual proof points

## What to avoid
- Don't conflate personal banking with business banking features — keep retail copy focused on consumer everyday banking
- Avoid leading with features; the consumer angle is about the experience and the relationship outcome
- Em-dashes
