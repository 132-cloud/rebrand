# Creative Brief — Privacy Policy

**Page:** /privacy-policy
**In one line:** A legal disclosure page explaining how Nymbus collects, uses, and protects visitor data on nymbus.com.

## What this page is for
Compliance and legal disclosure. This page exists to fulfill regulatory and platform requirements for data transparency — not to convert visitors. The only actions are opt-out links for Google Analytics and third-party cookies. Primary audience: users who clicked a privacy link in a footer, email, or cookie banner.

## Who it's for
Primary: Website visitors, email subscribers, and compliance reviewers checking Nymbus's data practices. Secondary: legal and privacy teams at prospective enterprise clients doing vendor diligence. Visitors arrive with a specific question about data use — they want a fast, accurate answer.

## What we're communicating
- Nymbus only uses name and email for informational or marketing email — and will never share it with third parties
- Google Analytics, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Adaptive network pixels are used for anonymous tracking and remarketing
- No PII is stored in cookies
- Users can opt out of Google's cookies and third-party ad cookies via provided links
- DPO contact: compliance@nymbus.com
- Consent notice: visiting the site constitutes consent to tracking under this policy

## Tone & feel
Plain and factual. This is a legal utility page — it should be readable and organized, not styled like a product page. Consistent with the overall site typographic system but no brand expression needed beyond basic layout.

## Must-haves / non-negotiables
- "We will never share your information with any third party at any time" — this is a bold, prominent commitment in the current page; preserve it with emphasis
- Opt-out links: Google Analytics opt-out (https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout) and NAI opt-out (https://optout.networkadvertising.org/) must be live and functional
- DPO contact (compliance@nymbus.com) must be a live mailto link
- The all-caps consent paragraph must be preserved verbatim
- Two section headings: "Your Information" and "Tracking Visits to Our Website"

## Interactive / visual elements
- No interactive elements needed beyond standard in-text hyperlinks for opt-out URLs and DPO email
- Page layout: standard hero + long-form prose body, consistent with site container width and prose styling

## What to avoid
- Don't redesign this into a complex multi-section layout — clarity and findability are the only goals
- Don't remove or soften the third-party data sharing commitment — it's a stated brand promise
- Em-dashes
