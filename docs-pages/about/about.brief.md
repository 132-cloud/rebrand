# Creative Brief — About

**Page:** /about
**In one line:** The About page establishes Nymbus's identity, leadership credibility, and investor backing for prospects doing due diligence.

## What this page is for
Late-consideration and due-diligence stage. Prospects evaluating Nymbus as a vendor want to know who's behind the platform, who funds it, and whether the leadership team has credible banking and operator experience. Secondary audience: press, analysts, and partners. Primary action: contact or explore the platform.

## Who it's for
Primary: Decision-makers at financial institutions (CEO, CTO, COO) doing vendor diligence before a demo or contract conversation. Secondary: journalists, analysts, and industry partners assessing Nymbus's legitimacy and backer quality. Visitors arrive with some awareness of the brand; they're vetting, not discovering.

## What we're communicating
- Nymbus is the connective tissue between banking infrastructure, operations, and growth strategy
- The leadership team spans banking, product, technology, risk, revenue, and AI — not a pure tech startup
- The board brings both banking operators and institutional investors
- Investors include named banks and credit unions as well as Insight Partners — the platform is backed by its own customers

## Tone & feel
Grounded and substantive. This page should feel like the "Built for Builders" chapter of the brand — operators talking to operators. Avoid corporate boilerplate; let the team grid and investor logos carry the credibility rather than over-writing it.

## Must-haves / non-negotiables
- Full leadership grid with photos and titles (10 people)
- Board of directors grid with photos and affiliations (5 people)
- Investor logo grid — all 9 investors listed with logos
- Hero statement sets the brand philosophy, not a product pitch

## Interactive / visual elements
- Leadership and board grids: headshots with name and title on hover or below image
- Investor grid: logos at consistent scale with graceful fallback to text if image fails to load

## What to avoid
- Avoid turning this into a product page — no feature descriptions or platform capabilities beyond the hero statement
- No generic "we're passionate about banking" copy
- Em-dashes
